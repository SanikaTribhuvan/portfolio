Selected Publications

Research in Print

01
Published · Scopus-Indexed

Phantom Ledger: A Cryptographically Anchored Forensic Audit System for Institutional Data Integrity

ICIISTM 2026 — International Conference on Inventions, Innovations in Science, Technology & Management
Best Paper Award 1st Prize Solo-Authored Scopus Proceedings

Phantom Ledger proposes a tamper-evident audit architecture for institutional databases using cryptographic chaining, anomaly detection, and explainable AI. The system makes data manipulation detectable, traceable, and cryptographically deniable — independently developed and presented at ICIISTM 2026, March.

02
Published · IJAAR

GlitchZero: Language-Agnostic AI Middleware for Real-Time Data Integrity Verification

IJAAR — International Journal of Advanced Academic Research
Language-Agnostic Real-Time Middleware Architecture

GlitchZero introduces an AI-driven middleware layer capable of intercepting and verifying data streams in real-time, independent of the underlying programming language or system stack. The approach enables integrity guarantees in heterogeneous production environments without requiring source-level instrumentation.

03
Under Review

FedCohort: Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning for Hantavirus-Inspired Epidemiological Modelling

Manuscript under review — 2026
Federated Learning Differential Privacy Public Health AI

FedCohort adapts federated learning with differential privacy guarantees to epidemiological outbreak modelling, drawing on Hantavirus transmission dynamics as a case study. The architecture allows multi-institutional collaboration without centralised data exposure — a privacy-first approach to public health intelligence.

Built solo at CASAS · New Arts College, Ahilyanagar

Speaking & Events

In the Room

01
Gramin AI Summit

An invitation to speak at a grassroots AI event aimed at bringing artificial intelligence literacy to rural communities in Maharashtra. Preparing the talk forced me to strip away jargon and speak to the core of what this technology means for people living outside tech hubs.

The questions from the audience were the most grounding I have received. They did not ask about accuracy benchmarks. They asked about trust.

02
Conferences & Research Events

Presenting at ICIISTM 2026, ICADMA 2025, NCRT AIML, COGNOTSAV, and TECH-EXPO taught me that the room matters. You are not defending a paper. You are making a case for why the problem matters.

Solo entries at every venue. The solitude was not a disadvantage — it was clarifying. You learn to trust your own reasoning when there is nobody else to defer to.

03
Music & Stage Performances

Piano and vocals at Blossom 2K26 — our college annual fest. Performance anxiety for a research presentation and performance anxiety for a stage act feel different, but they have the same cure: preparation until the fear runs out of room.

Music teaches what research sometimes forgets: that the feeling of a thing is as important as the correctness of a thing.

Community Engagement

People For Animals
Ahilyanagar

Animal welfare did not feel like a cause I chose — it felt like something I stumbled into and could not walk away from. People For Animals, Ahilyanagar gave me a way to act on that.

I joined as a volunteer, showed up for awareness drives, and helped document cases. The work is unglamorous and often distressing. But it is also the most unambiguous thing I do — there is no publication to chase, no prize to win. Just the immediate problem of an animal in need and the people trying to help it.

Research asks you to think in systems. Community work asks you to act on the individual case in front of you. Both are necessary. I find they make each other better.
